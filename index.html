MD Anderson and Rice University

While pursuing a bachelor's degree in psychology at Rice University, Derek worked part-time at MD Anderson Cancer Center in the biostatistics department. Derek's role was to design and develop software for clinicians to assess endpoints in clinical cancer trials based on Bayesian monitoring criteria. This was done under the direction of Dr. Peter Thall. Other statistical challenges included estimation of error rates in fluorescent in-situ hybridization studies, for which Derek received publication credit in the British Journal of Statistics. At Rice, publications in eyewitness testimony and memory were also obtained.

Compaq Computer Corporation

As an intern while at Rice, and following graduation, Derek worked at Compaq Computer Corporation. Initially in the human factors department, where his primary role was the development of a consumer-focused product called MediaPilot, which was the first comprehensive suite of multi-media editing and playback tools to ship on a consumer PC. Following this, Derek moved to the communications group at Compaq and shipped telephony hardware to support the Microsoft Taz telephony server.
